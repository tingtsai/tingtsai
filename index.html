--- layout: home title: About Ching-Ting Tsai fontsize: 12pt --- I'm a Ph.D. student in [Prof. Bianxiao Cui's lab](https://cuilab.stanford.edu) at Stanford University. My research aims to ansewer key questions at the nano-bio interface, including protein sensing and cullular electrophysiology. As for the protein sensing direction, In this direction, I spefically involve in engineering a in vitro nano-sensing platform for evaluating both positive and negative curvature sensing proteins. Recent work in the Cui Lab demonstrated that some proteins preferentially accumulate at nanostructure locations in a curvature-dependent manner, and this accumulation stimulates intracellular prcess such as actin polymerization and endocytosis. I also involve intensively in developing and characterizing high throughput electrophysiology tools including nanoelectrode array (NEA) and mesh electronics. We develop nanoscale electronics, minimally invasive, solid-state electrical probes to achieve high fidelity recording for cardiomyocytes and neurons. I was borned and raised in Taipei, Taiwan. I received my B.S. in Chemsitry from National Taiwan University in 2017. I partook research in Genomics Research Center at Academia Sinica to study sulfatase-1 inhibitor for osteoarthritis. My research advisor was Prof. Shang-Cheng Hung. I love scientific writing.
